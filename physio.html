System Architecture & Methodology

1. Basal Metabolic Rate

Uses the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation — the current gold standard for metabolic estimation:

BMR = (10 × W) + (6.25 × H) − (5 × A) + 5

2. TDEE Calculation

Activity multiplier applied to BMR to account for the Thermogenic Effect of Activity:

  • Sedentary → BMR × 1.2
  • Moderate → BMR × 1.55
  • Athlete → BMR × 1.9

3. Protein — Lean Mass Logic

Protein scaled from Lean Body Mass to optimise power-to-weight ratio for calisthenics athletes:

Target = LBM (kg) × 2.2g

4. Phase Differentials

Lean Bulk: +10% surplus for muscle synthesis without excess fat storage.

Lean Cut: −15% deficit for fat oxidation while preserving neural strength output.

Glossary of Terms
  • BMR — Calories burned at complete rest to sustain vital functions.
  • TDEE — Total daily calories burned including all physical activity.
  • Lean Body Mass — Total bodyweight minus fat mass.
  • Navy Formula — Body fat estimation using circumference measurements.
  • TEF — Thermic Effect of Food; calories burned during digestion.
  • NEAT — Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis; incidental movement energy.
Macronutrient Distribution

Recommended intake ranges for general athletic performance. Calisthenics athletes typically bias toward higher protein and moderate carbohydrates.

Protein 10–35% Carbs 45–65% Fats 20–35%

A 3,500 kcal deficit ≈ 1 lb of fat lost. A safe sustainable deficit is 500–1,000 kcal/day. Focus on whole food sources and keep cholesterol below 300mg/day.
