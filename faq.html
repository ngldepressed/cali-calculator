How often should I take a deload week?
Every 6th week is the recommended protocol. During this period, you should reduce your volume by 50% (fewer sets/reps) but keep the intensity high (same weight or progression level). This allows the nervous system and connective tissues to recover without losing the "feeling" of heavy loading.
Do bicep curls help with the Planche?
Partially correct. While the Planche is a pushing skill, the biceps contract isometrically as elbow stabilizers during straight-arm loading. Well-conditioned elbow flexors (including the biceps) contribute to joint stability and load tolerance, which may help reduce stress on the distal biceps tendon during high-tension holds when properly progressed.
What is the 'SAID' principle?
SAID stands for Specific Adaptation to Imposed Demands. It means your body only gets better at exactly what you practice. If you want a Full Planche, training high-rep pushups won't help; you must practice the specific mechanical demand of the planche (e.g., holding a tuck or advanced tuck).
Can I train Planche and Front Lever on the same day?
Yes, but it is better to prioritize one. Train your "weak" skill first while the CNS is fresh. Combining them is efficient as they use antagonistic muscle groups (Push vs. Pull), but high-intensity holds require maximum neural drive which diminishes as the session progresses.

Why has my pull-up progress plateaued?
Usually a lack of "Scapular Depression" strength. If your lats are strong but your scapula can't stay depressed under load, you lose the mechanical advantage needed for the top half of the pull. Solution: Add heavy weighted scapular pulls and focus on keeping shoulders away from ears.
Why do my elbows hurt during Planche or Back Lever training?
This is typically Distal Biceps Tendonitis or "Medial Epicondylitis." It occurs because the lever arm is too long for your current tendon density.

Bio-Correction: Switch to a regressed version (like Tuck or Leans) immediately. Incorporate high-rep (20+) light bicep curls and "Zanetti Presses" to pump blood into the tendons for repair. Never train through sharp elbow pain.
Can you build a massive physique with only Calisthenics?
Absolutely. While bodybuilders use isolation, calisthenics builds muscle through Compound Overload.

To maximize hypertrophy (growth), you must focus on Weighted Calisthenics in the 8-12 rep range. Adding weight to pull-ups and dips is the most efficient way to build a "V-taper" back and thick triceps without a gym.
Should I train Calisthenics every day?
For elite skills, training every day is counter-productive. High-tension statics fry the Central Nervous System (CNS).

Optimal Protocol: 3 to 4 high-intensity sessions per week. On "off" days, focus on mobility and active recovery. Your muscles grow and your tendons strengthen while you rest, not while you train.
