ZERO TO ADVANCED TUCK:
PLANCHE PROGRESSION

STOP BEING A HUMAN PENDULUM // DEFY PHYSICS

"The planche isn’t a 'move.' It’s a straight-arm strength feat that will absolutely ruin you if you treat it with anything less than total respect."

This isn't about "trying hard"; it's about forcing your biology to adapt to a load it was never intended to carry. You are essentially trying to turn your body into a cantilever bridge made of meat and bone.

SYSTEM_WARNING: If you don’t respect the mechanics, you’ll just get a chronic case of tendonitis and a permanent seat on the sidelines.

Phase I: The Non-Negotiables

1. Protraction: The "Angry Cat"

Push the floor away like you’re trying to shove it into the basement. You want your shoulder blades moving as far away from each other as possible. If you lose this, your skeletal structure loses its lock, and you’re suddenly trying to hold your entire body weight with tiny stabilizer muscles.

2. Depression: Shoulders Out of Your Ears

Shrugging kills your leverage. You need to pull your shoulders down toward your butt. Think about tucking your shoulder blades into your back pockets. This creates a solid "shelf" for your weight to sit on.

3. Straight Arms: The "Bone Lock"

The second your elbows micro-bend, the load shifts from your connective tissue to your triceps. Your triceps are weak compared to the forces at play here. Lock the elbows. Imagine they are made of solid steel.

4. External Rotation: Screw into the Floor

Turn your hands out 30–45°. While they’re glued to the floor, rotate your elbow pits toward the front. This "screws" the humerus into the shoulder socket, protecting your bicep tendons.

Phase II: The Frog Trap

The "Frog Stand" is a balance trick. Your knees are resting on your triceps, meaning you aren't "holding" yourself up. To bridge the neurological gap, use the Alternating Frog Release:

  • Step 1 Get into a Frog Stand.
  • Step 2 Lift one knee off your elbow. Keep it tucked but not touching.
  • Step 3 Hold for 5 seconds, then switch.

Phase III: The Advanced Tuck

In the Advanced Tuck, you’re trying to become a beam. Gravity starts to really hate you here.

{Total Body Tension} = {Glute Squeeze} + {Core Compression}

The "Open the Hip" Drill

To get to the Advanced Tuck, you need a flat back. Pull your butt up and back until your spine is a flat table. The Counter-Lean: As your butt moves back, your shoulders must lean further forward to compensate. It’s a seesaw.

Phase IV: Tendon Conditioning

Your muscles are fast. Your tendons are slow and stupid. You need to build armor.

THE PELICAN PREP: Take light weights or a band. Put your arms behind your back, keep them perfectly straight, and lift them up. High reps, low weight. Conditioning the "pull" on the tendon.

Final Safety Checklist

  • Protocol: CNS Max effort hold of 5s? Rest for at least 2 minutes. Quality over junk reps.
  • Pain Audit Muscle burn is good. Sharp, stabbing pain in the joint is a full STOP.
  • Regress If your back isn't flat in the Advanced Tuck, go back to Tuck. Do not polish a statue that hasn't been carved yet.
