Specificity is King

Engine Strength vs. Structural Integrity

"Most people in calisthenics are strong. Very few are skilled."

You can bang out 5x5 weighted dips with 20kg hanging from your waist. You can crank weighted pull-ups like a machine. Your chest is pumped, your lats are thick, your triceps look like horseshoes.

And then you lean forward for a planche... and your body folds like a lawn chair.

The General Strength Illusion: You built a bigger engine. You never built the chassis.

The Planche: The Brutal Teacher

The planche is unforgiving. You don’t grind a planche rep. You either hold it or you don’t. If your shoulder blades pinch together, you’re not doing a planche — you’re doing a heavy push-up fantasy.

Weekly Protocol

  • Frequency: 2–3 times per week. (Connective tissue needs 48-72h recovery).
  • Total Volume: 60–120 seconds of quality hold time per session.

The Front Lever: Less Ego, More Precision

The Golden Rules of Skill Training

1. The 3-Minute Rest Rule

You’re training the CNS (Central Nervous System). If you don’t let it recover, you get weaker set by set. Resting 60 seconds isn't "hardcore"—it's underperforming.

2. Failure is Not Noble

The second your arms bend, your scapula collapses, or your hips drop, the set is over. You don’t earn points for suffering through bad mechanics; you earn tendonitis.

3. Deload or Break

Every 4th or 5th week, cut your volume by 50% and drop to an easier progression. This allows the tendons to catch up to the muscles.
